Plant Biology - GTA Spring 2018

As a Graduate Teaching Assistant (GTA) for the BIOL 150 - World of Plants Lab, I instructed weekly lab sessions with SFSU undergraduate students.

Topics covered in the lab included plant systematics, cellular structures, anatomy and morphology, ecology, and evolution.

I also led the students on 5 instructional field trips to ecologically significant locations in San Francisco. Throughout the semester we visited Golden Gate Park, Lake Merced, Fort Funston, Glen Canyon Park, and Alemany Farm!

Although I do not have expertise in plant biology, this topic was one of my favorites during my undergraduate biology courses. Fun fact: Many crop species are polyploid, meaning that each cell contains more than two complete sets of chromosomes. Wheat is commonly hexaploid, strawberries octaploid, and some species of sugarcane dodecaploid - 12 complete sets of chromosomes! An animal could never pull that off.
